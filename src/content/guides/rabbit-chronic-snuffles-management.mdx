if your rabbit has been diagnosed with snuffles, the hard truth is that for many rabbits it becomes a lifelong condition rather than a one-off illness. in Singapore, this is especially challenging. the combination of year-round heat, humidity hovering between 70 and 90 percent, and a relatively small pool of exotic-trained vets means managing a chronic respiratory condition requires more discipline than it would in a temperate country. HDB flat living adds another layer: ventilation is often limited, shared corridor air quality varies, and running a dehumidifier or AC unit around the clock becomes the difference between a stable rabbit and one in constant relapse. this guide covers what long-term management actually looks like for SG owners.

## understanding chronic snuffles

snuffles is caused most often by Pasteurella multocida bacteria, though Bordetella and Staphylococcus are also implicated. a single antibiotic course may clear visible symptoms, but the bacteria often persist in the nasal passages, tear ducts, and dental roots. this is why "my rabbit was treated and seemed fine, then relapsed six months later" is so common.

"chronic" means the infection is not fully eradicated. it can remain dormant for months, then flare when your rabbit is stressed, overheated, or immunocompromised. in Singapore's climate, heat stress alone is a common trigger. understanding this helps you shift your mindset from "curing" snuffles to "managing" it. that shift changes how you track symptoms, schedule vet visits, and set up your home.

## controlling your SG environment

the single biggest lever you have in SG is temperature and humidity control. Pasteurella thrives when rabbits are heat-stressed. keeping your rabbit's space at 18 to 24 degrees Celsius is the standard welfare recommendation for rabbits generally; for a snuffles rabbit, it is non-negotiable.

if you cannot run AC in your rabbit's area 24 hours a day, consider a portable AC unit for their dedicated room, or at minimum a fan blowing across a frozen water bottle combined with ceramic floor tiles to dissipate heat. do not rely on open windows in Singapore. outdoor air here is warm, humid, and often carries pollutants and haze particulates that aggravate respiratory conditions.

humidity matters just as much as temperature. a dehumidifier rated for a small room, roughly 12 to 16 litres per day capacity, can bring relative humidity from 80 percent down to 55 to 60 percent. at those levels your rabbit's respiratory system works significantly less hard. as of 2026, a basic dehumidifier in SG typically costs around SGD 120 to 280.

avoid strong scents in the home. incense, scented candles, aerosol sprays, and cigarette smoke all irritate compromised nasal passages. if you live in an HDB unit where neighbours burn incense in the corridor, seal the gap under your front door with a draught strip and keep your rabbit's room door closed during those periods.

## tracking symptoms day to day

consistent daily observation is what separates owners who catch flare-ups early from those who end up at an emergency vet on a Sunday night. every day, check for:

- nasal discharge. clear and watery is lower concern; thick, white, yellow, or green discharge warrants a call to your vet
- front paw fur matting. rabbits wipe their noses with their paws. matted fur on the inside of both front legs is a reliable early signal of increased nasal discharge
- breathing pattern. any audible wheezing, clicking, or laboured breathing at rest is urgent
- appetite and pellet intake. a drop in food interest often precedes visible respiratory symptoms by 24 to 48 hours
- eye discharge. snuffles bacteria frequently travel from nasal passages to tear ducts; weepy or crusty eyes are a related warning sign

keep a simple daily log, even a brief note in your phone. when you visit the vet, that history is far more useful than memory alone. your vet can see trends that a single consultation snapshot misses.

> **urgent:** audible wheezing, open-mouth breathing, or blue-tinged gums require same-day vet contact. do not wait and see.

## working with your vet long-term

chronic snuffles management is a partnership between you and a SG exotic vet. the key is to establish a consistent relationship with one clinic rather than hopping between different vets at every flare-up. continuity matters because your vet can track culture results, antibiotic history, and response patterns over time.

a typical maintenance schedule for a stable chronic snuffles rabbit is a check-up every three to four months, with a bacterial swab culture run annually or whenever symptoms change character. as of 2026, a standard exotic rabbit consultation in SG typically ranges from SGD 50 to 120, and a bacterial culture with sensitivity testing can add SGD 80 to 180 to that cost.

culture and sensitivity testing matters more for snuffles than for almost any other rabbit condition. Pasteurella can develop resistance to common antibiotics like enrofloxacin after repeated treatment cycles. a sensitivity test tells your vet which antibiotic will actually work this time, rather than prescribing by habit. if your rabbit has been treated multiple times in the past two years, push for a fresh culture before starting another course.

do not stop antibiotics early when symptoms clear. this is the primary driver of antibiotic resistance in chronic cases. complete the full prescribed course even when your rabbit looks and acts completely normal.

## nutrition and immune support

a rabbit's immune resilience is closely tied to gut health. the foundation is a high-quality diet: unlimited timothy or orchard grass hay, limited pellets (roughly half a tablespoon per kilogram of body weight per day for adults), and daily fresh leafy greens.

gut motility slows when rabbits are ill or under stress. if your snuffles rabbit is on a course of antibiotics, monitor litter output closely. a reduction in caecotropes or faecal pellets signals disrupted gut flora. some owners add a rabbit-safe probiotic during antibiotic courses; discuss this with your vet before starting, as not all probiotic formulations are appropriate for rabbits.

be sceptical of any product marketed specifically as a "respiratory immunity booster" or "snuffles supplement." no supplement has demonstrated reliable efficacy for preventing Pasteurella flare-ups. your money is better spent on proper AC and vet check-ups.

keep your rabbit's body condition stable. an underweight snuffles rabbit has fewer reserves during a flare. aim for a body condition score of 3 out of 5 as a rough ongoing target, adjustable with your vet's input.

## dental health and its link to snuffles

this connection surprises many owners. the roots of a rabbit's upper cheek teeth sit directly adjacent to the nasal passages. when dental disease causes root elongation or abscess, it can either trigger snuffles or make an existing case dramatically harder to control.

if your rabbit's snuffles seems treatment-resistant, ask your exotic vet to assess dental health, including skull X-rays or a CT scan if warranted. as of 2026, skull radiographs in SG typically range from SGD 150 to 350 depending on the clinic and equipment. this is worth investigating before cycling through more antibiotics.

regular hay consumption is your first line of dental maintenance. hay wears down molar teeth naturally. if your snuffles rabbit is eating less hay due to nasal congestion affecting appetite, this creates a secondary dental risk you need to monitor.

## what owners often get wrong

**stopping treatment when symptoms improve.** the most common mistake across the board. nasal discharge clears up, the rabbit seems fine, and the owner stops antibiotics on day 7 of a 14-day course. this guarantees a faster and harder relapse, and accelerates resistance development. always complete the full prescribed course.

**treating every flare identically.** what worked 18 months ago may not work today because the bacterial strain has shifted or resistance has developed. a flare after a long stable period warrants a fresh culture, not just a repeat of the last prescription.

**skipping vet check-ins during stable periods.** when your rabbit has been well for six months, it is tempting to cancel the routine check-up. but stable periods are when your vet establishes the baselines that matter when something changes: weight, lung sounds, nasal cavity appearance. do not skip these.

**assuming the environment is adequate.** in Singapore, "I leave the fan on" is not sufficient climate control for a chronic snuffles rabbit. if your rabbit is having frequent flare-ups despite consistent medication compliance, revisit temperature, humidity, and air quality before assuming the antibiotic is failing.
