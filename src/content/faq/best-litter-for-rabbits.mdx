## the simple answer

paper-based recycled litter is the standard recommendation for pet rabbits.

it is:

- non-toxic if ingested (rabbits often nibble small amounts)
- low-dust (important for respiratory health)
- absorbs urine well
- widely available at SG supermarkets and pet shops
- biodegradable

brands you will see in SG include Yesterday's News, Carefresh, and various store-brand equivalents.

## acceptable alternatives

**compressed wood pellet litter**: often sold for cats, made from softwood pressed into small pellets. cheaper per kilogram than paper-based. ensure it is plain wood with no chemical additives. avoid cedar.

**aspen shavings**: safe but dustier than paper. acceptable in a pinch.

**hay over a thin paper layer**: some owners use a thin layer of paper-based litter with hay on top. rabbits love eating while they urinate, so this dual-purpose setup works. just plan to replace hay frequently.

## what to avoid

**clumping clay cat litter**: toxic if ingested. rabbits often eat hay near their litter box, and ingestion of clumping litter can cause GI blockages. never use.

**cedar shavings**: release aromatic oils that irritate rabbit airways and can affect the liver. never use.

**pine shavings (untreated)**: same issue as cedar; respiratory and liver concerns. avoid.

**scented litters of any kind**: artificial fragrances are respiratory irritants. avoid.

**newspaper alone**: low absorbency, not effective. use proper litter.

**corn-cob litter**: can swell if eaten and cause GI blockage. avoid.

## how much litter to use

most rabbit litter boxes work with 2 to 3 cm of litter at the bottom. some owners use less because hay is placed on top. experiment with your rabbit's preferences.

## how often to change

- **spot-clean daily**: remove obvious large piles, replace urine-soaked sections, replenish hay on top
- **full change weekly**: empty the box, wash with mild soap and water, refill with fresh litter
- **deeper clean monthly**: scrub the box thoroughly

a smelly box is a rejected box. if you notice the rabbit avoiding the litter box, increase cleaning frequency before assuming a behaviour problem.

## hay placement

place a hay rack directly over or next to the litter box. rabbits like to eat hay while they urinate, which triples the time they spend on the box and reinforces litter training. see our [litter training guide](/care/litter-training-rabbit-hdb/).

## SG-specific notes

litter is widely available at NTUC, Sheng Siong, and most SG pet shops. prices vary; pet specialty shops may charge premium for the same product available cheaper at supermarkets.

if you have multiple boxes for free-range areas, consistency of litter type matters. rabbits associate the texture and smell with the bathroom area; mixing types can confuse them.

## the multi-rabbit consideration

bonded pairs sometimes share a box, sometimes have separate preferences. provide one or two boxes per rabbit until you see which they use, then consolidate or expand based on observed preference.
